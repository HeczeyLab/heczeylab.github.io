Meet our team!

Andras Heczey, MD

Principal Investigator

Dr. Heczey is a principal investigator and Scientific Director of Translational Research at Seattle Children's Research Institute. He is also a Professor of Pediatrics at the University of Washington. His work focuses on developing innovative novel treatments for pediatric patients with solid tumors like neuroblastoma and liver cancers. As a member of the multidisciplinary Solid Tumor Team at SCRI, his work focuses on redirecting the immune system, primarily by engineering CAR T cells, to attack cancer cells.

Our lab takes a multi-pronged approach when it comes to assessing the longevity and efficacy of CAR T cells. In addition to elucidating the underlying immunological effects of various CAR T cell enhancements, we strive to develop the best therapies for pediatric oncology patients, meaning that we are constantly analyzing data from various sources – in vitro, in vivo, in silico, and various phases of our ongoing clinical trials.

In his free time he enjoys spending time with his family, swimming, and cycling various trails across Seattle and the regions throughout the Pacific Northwest.

Michelle Choe, MD

Assistant Professor, Clinical Research Division – Fred Hutch

My journey as a pediatric oncologist and blood and marrow transplanter has been greatly influenced by my personal experience being diagnosed with osteosarcoma as a young adult. While I was quite fortunate to have an excellent prognosis, I wrestled with the question of “Why me?” which transitioned “Why not me?” The answers to these questions have fueled my motivation for finding better ways to help those with worse prognosis.

I aim to help test novel cellular therapies for treatment of pediatric solid tumors. I currently serve as the PI for a phase 1 clinical trial evaluating FOLR1-CAR T cells in relapsed/refractory osteosarcoma, as well as a phase 1 clinical trial evaluating IL15/IL21 armored GPC3-CAR T cells for relapsed/refractory GPC3+ solid tumors.
